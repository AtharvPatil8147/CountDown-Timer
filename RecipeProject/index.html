Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • Oil for frying

Recipe

  1. Mix milk powder, flour, and baking soda in a bowl. Add milk slowly and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  2. Cover and rest the dough for 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water. Add cardamom and rose water. Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Make small balls from the dough without cracks.
  5. Heat oil on medium flame and fry the balls until golden brown.
  6. Soak the fried balls in warm syrup for at least 1 hour before serving.

Rasgulla

Ingredients

  • 1 litre full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tsp rose water or cardamom powder (optional)

Recipe

  1. Boil milk and add lemon juice to curdle it.
  2. Strain the paneer using a muslin cloth and rinse under cold water.
  3. Press out excess water and knead for 8–10 minutes to get a smooth dough.
  4. Divide into small smooth balls with no cracks.
  5. In a pot, boil sugar and water to make syrup.
  6. Drop the balls into boiling syrup, cover, and cook on high flame for 10–12 minutes.
  7. Let them rest in the syrup for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Kheer (Rice Pudding)

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup basmati rice
  • 1 litre full cream milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 5-6 cardamom pods
  • 10-12 saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped almonds, pistachios, and raisins

Recipe

  1. Wash and soak rice for 30 minutes. Drain the water.
  2. Boil milk in a heavy-bottomed pan and add soaked rice.
  3. Cook on low flame, stirring often, until rice is soft and milk thickens.
  4. Add sugar, cardamom, and saffron. Stir well.
  5. Add chopped dry fruits and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  6. Serve warm or chilled.

Besan Ladoo

Ingredients

  • 2 cups gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (optional)

Recipe

  1. Heat ghee in a pan. Add besan and roast on low flame until golden and aromatic (about 15–20 mins).
  2. Let the mixture cool slightly (not fully).
  3. Add powdered sugar, cardamom powder, and chopped nuts. Mix thoroughly.
  4. Shape into small ladoos while the mixture is still warm.
  5. Let them cool completely and store in an airtight container.

Coconut Barfi

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated fresh coconut
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Ghee (for greasing)

Recipe

  1. In a heavy pan, mix coconut, sugar, and milk.
  2. Cook on low flame, stirring continuously, until it thickens and leaves the pan sides.
  3. Add cardamom powder and mix well.
  4. Grease a tray with ghee and pour the mixture into it. Flatten evenly.
  5. Let it cool slightly, then cut into pieces and allow to set.
